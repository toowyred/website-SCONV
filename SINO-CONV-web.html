How to use

Case

Case buttons work with Latin-based characters that have upper and lower case forms. The 全と角カナ button toggles between fullwidth (全角 カナ) and halfwidth (半角 ｶﾅ) for katakana and ASCII characters.

Glyph converter

Type or paste text into the input buffer, then click a converter button. Conversions work in any direction — the input does not need to be in a specific script. For example, you can convert Japanese Kanji directly to Korean Hanja, or Simplified Chinese to Kanji.

  • 中 Traditionalconverts to Traditional Chinese (正體字).
    Changed characters are highlighted in red
  • 中 Simplifiedconverts to Simplified Chinese (简化字).
    Changed characters are highlighted in green
  • 日 Kanjiconverts to Japanese Kanji (新字体 shinjitai).
    Changed characters are highlighted in pink
  • 韓 Hanjaconverts to Korean Hanja with reform glyph variants (敎→教, 硏→研, 爲→为, etc.).
    Changed characters are highlighted in blue

When the target script has multiple possible glyph variants for a character, the converter follows each standard's preferred form — e.g. Japanese Kanji uses shinjitai (新字体), Korean Hanja uses reform glyphs adopted in modern usage.

Due to the aesthetic design and to maintain the use of one font across entire webpage, some characters may look or be displayed the same.
Please use the Change font button in the editor header and zoom if needed to inspect differences.

HánTự — Chữ Nôm lookup

This button works differently from the others, because 𡨸喃 — the historical Vietnamese logographic script — was never fully standardized before the switch to Quốc Ngữ. Countless variant forms exist, many of which cannot even display without specialized fonts (we use「字」as a stand-in for our button because the Vietnamese-equivalent 「𡨸」requires rare Unicode extensions).

To use: highlight exactly one Han character in the input buffer, then click 越 HánTự. This opens an external lookup page (hvdic.thivien.net) for that character's Nôm readings and variants.

Reading buttons

The pronunciation buttons on the left side of the editor output character-by-character readings:

  • 繁Bopomofo / Zhuyin (注音符號)
  • 简Pīnyīn with tone marks
  • 日Japanese on'yomi in hiragana
  • 韓Korean hanja reading in hangul
  • 越Sino-Vietnamese (Hán Việt) reading

Sources

  • OpenCC-js (v1.0.5) — Open Chinese Convert by nk2028. Handles all script conversions between Traditional, Simplified, Japanese Kanji, and Korean Hanja offline.
  • Unihan readings — Character readings (Mandarin pinyin, Japanese on'yomi, Korean hangul, Vietnamese Hán Việt) extracted from the Unicode Han Database via dahlia/unihan-json (Unicode 12.1.0).
